# 我为儿子写的一本书

我儿子今年14岁了。

这些年，每当他问我："爸，你到底是干什么的？"我总是不知道怎么回答。我会说点什么"研究大脑怎么做决定"之类的话，
但是显然这不是很让人满意。

于是，我决定来写一本书，以后他再问我这个问题，我可以把书递到他手里说：***喏。这就是我做的事。***

我是一名脑科学研究者。我和我的团队，把一个个白天——还有很多个夜晚——花在研究大脑上：人怎么做决定，为什么会犹豫，为什么会相信直觉，为什么情绪会绑架理性，为什么记忆会悄悄改写未来。

可所有这些知识，都被锁在专业期刊和术语后面。不像许多有些学科，神经科学的理论和成果太碎片化了，普通人很难理解，也不容易搞清楚我们在做什么。那些最可能从这门科学中受益的人，反而离它最远。

为大众写作，原来是一件很可怕的事。科学家被训练成凡事都要加限定、加保留、加脚注。要把一个故事讲好，又不丢掉真相，是另一种本事，我得从头学起。每当卡住的时候，我都会问自己一个简单的问题：*我儿子会看得明白吗？* 如果有一天他读完它，合上书，心里想着：*"哦——原来爸爸做的是这个"*——那么，我的目的就达到了。

所以就有了这本书：《纠结的大脑》。

# A Book for My son

My son is 14 this year.

Over the years, whenever he asked me, "Dad, what do you actually do?" I never quite knew how to answer. I'd say something like "I study how the brain makes decisions" — but clearly, that wasn't a very satisfying reply.

So I decided to write a book. Next time he asks, I can simply hand it to him and say: Here. This is what I do.

I'm a brain scientist. My team and I spend our days — and many of our nights — studying the brain: how people make decisions, why we hesitate, why we trust our intuition, why emotion hijacks reason, and why memory quietly rewrites the future.

But all of this knowledge is locked away behind academic journals and technical jargon. Unlike many other fields, neuroscience is fragmented — its theories and findings are hard for ordinary readers to piece together, and it's not easy for outsiders to understand what we're really doing. The people who could benefit most from this science are often the ones furthest from it.

Writing for a general audience, it turns out, is terrifying. Scientists are trained to qualify everything — to hedge, to footnote, to leave room for doubt. Telling a good story without sacrificing the truth is a different skill entirely, and one I had to learn from scratch. Whenever I got stuck, I'd ask myself a simple question: Would my son understand this? If one day he finishes the book, closes it, and thinks, "Oh — so that's what Dad does," — then I'll have done what I set out to do.

And so, this book came to be: The Entangled Brain.
