# A Science Outreach Trip to Kashgar: 

I recently wrapped up my educational science outreach journey across Kashgar, where I 
worked as a member of Shanghai scientist team and delivered lectures on neuroscience 
and decision-making to students from three local middle schools. This short trip left 
me with countless warm memories that I still revisit with a full heart.

From the moment I stepped into every classroom, I was instantly touched by the students’ 
boundless energy and genuine curiosity. All the kids were incredibly lively, adorable, 
and brimming with enthusiasm for science. Whenever I explained brain mechanisms or shared 
how our brains shape daily decision-making, they raised their hands nonstop, asking 
thoughtful, creative questions without hesitation. Their eager eyes and bold willingness
 to engage made every minute of my lectures worthwhile. It was such a moving experience 
 to witness young minds so hungry to explore the mysteries of neuroscience.
 
Apart from the rewarding time spent with the students, Kashgar’s natural treats 
also stole my heart. The local fruits here are unbelievably delicious—juicy, 
sweet, and full of rich, natural flavor. Even the fruit trees planted in the courtyard 
of my hotel were heavy with ripe fruits hanging down from every branch, a lovely, 
vivid sight unique to this land.

If I had to name two small regrets from this trip, there would be no hesitation at all. 
First, I didn’t get to chat with the students for nearly long enough. Between 
back-to-back lectures and limited break time, I barely scratched the surface of all 
the questions and stories they wanted to share with me. Second, I did not have enough 
time to experience Kashgar’s unique culture. I wish I had more time to experience Kashgar
in more depth.

This meaningful science trip to Kashgar has come to an end, but it is far from a final 
goodbye. I’m already looking forward to returning someday soon.
