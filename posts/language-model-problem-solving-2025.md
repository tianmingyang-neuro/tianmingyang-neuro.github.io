We are excited to share our latest paper, *"A language model of problem solving in humans and macaque monkeys"*, published in **Current Biology** and featured as the cover article.

## Summary

The paper examines whether transformer-based language models can capture the strategies that humans and macaques deploy when solving novel problems. We trained monkeys on a family of reasoning tasks and compared their behavioral signatures to those of fine-tuned LLMs.

### Key findings

- Both monkeys and LLMs show graded difficulty effects across problem classes.
- Attention heads in the transformer recapitulate the search-like strategies inferred from gaze.
- A simple in-context learning regime is sufficient to explain a surprising amount of variance.

> "The model isn't a brain — but it's a useful map of one corner of the cognitive landscape."
