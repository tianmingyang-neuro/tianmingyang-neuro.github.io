A recent flurry of papers has reopened the debate over what the OFC really represents. Is it value? Salience? Attention? Identity?

I think the most parsimonious reading of the data — including [our 2022 paper](#) — is that **the OFC represents multiple variables along approximately orthogonal axes**, and that the *apparent* dominance of one signal over another depends almost entirely on the task design.

## Why this matters

If you design a task in which value and salience covary, you will read out a "value" signal. If you orthogonalize them, the salience axis often dominates the variance. Neither result is wrong; they are projections of the same underlying geometry.

## A modest proposal

Future studies should report the full subspace decomposition — not just which variable explains the most variance — so that results across labs become directly comparable.
