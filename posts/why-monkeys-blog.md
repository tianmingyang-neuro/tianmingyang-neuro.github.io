People sometimes ask why, in the age of two-photon imaging in mice and ever-larger neural networks, we still bother to train monkeys.

## The short answer

Monkeys can do things mice can't, and they can tell us things models can't.

## A longer answer

Mice are wonderful for cellular and circuit-level questions. But many of the cognitive operations we care about — flexible rule use, deliberate planning, abstract reasoning — are either absent or hard to assay in rodents. The macaque sits in a sweet spot: complex enough to do the cognition, accessible enough to record from at single-cell resolution.

Models, meanwhile, give us *hypotheses* about computation. They cannot tell us which hypotheses biology actually implements. For that, you still need a brain.
